# TRP Outreach Draft

**To:** Raul Raymundo (CEO) or Mary Johnson (CDO), The Resurrection Project
**From:** Cruz Morales, TresPies
**Subject:** Displacement data for Pilsen — free pilot offer

---

Raul / Mary,

I lead TresPies, an AI research consultancy that builds spatial equity data infrastructure for CDCs. I've spent the last year building data systems at Commonwealth Development in Madison — census-tract-level analysis connecting transit access, housing affordability, health access, and school attendance into interactive tools that CWD uses for city council testimony and funder proposals.

I want to offer TRP a pilot engagement at no cost.

**The deliverable:** A Pilsen/Little Village/Back of the Yards displacement vulnerability map. Built on public data — HMDA lending patterns, ACS income and tenure shifts, Cook County Assessor values — overlaid against TRP's affordable housing footprint. Self-contained interactive viewer (single HTML file, no software to install, shareable via email). Usable for city council testimony, the Pilsen Stay in Place campaign, and funder presentations.

**Why free:** I'm building a methodology I want to prove transfers beyond one city. TRP is the right first partner — a faith-rooted CDC with deep community roots serving neighborhoods under active displacement pressure. If the pilot delivers value, I'd want to talk about an ongoing research partnership and a paid engagement to build out the full data infrastructure.

**What I'd need from TRP:**
- 1-hour conversation about which policy decisions the data needs to serve
- A list of TRP's properties (addresses, unit counts) — I'll map everything else from public sources
- Feedback on whether the outputs are useful for your advocacy

**Timeline:** 4-6 weeks from kickoff to deliverable.

I'd welcome 30 minutes to walk through what we built for CWD and discuss whether this is useful for TRP.
