# Rapid-Response Policy Brief Protocol

**[Organization Name] | Policy Analysis**
**Version 1.0**

---

## 72-Hour Production Timeline

### Hour 0-6: Trigger Identification and Scoping

- **Trigger event detected** (council vote scheduled, budget proposal released, state legislation filed, federal funding announcement, media coverage of equity issue)
- Research lead flags trigger and drafts 1-paragraph scope memo
- Organizational leadership approves scope or redirects within 2 hours
- Assign brief to appropriate template (zoning, education, transit, housing)

### Hour 6-24: Data Pull and Analysis

- Pull relevant data layers from equity data viewer
- Cross-reference with organization's portfolio data (if applicable)
- Generate tables, summary statistics, and map descriptions
- Identify 2-3 headline findings with equity implications
- Document all data sources and methodology

### Hour 24-48: Drafting and Internal Review

- Draft brief using the appropriate template (target: 2 pages, 800-1,000 words)
- Insert data visualizations or table placeholders
- Internal fact-check against primary data sources
- Tone review: confirm neutral, analytical framing throughout

### Hour 48-66: Quality Gates

1. **Fact-Check Gate** — Every statistic traced to a named source and verified
2. **Tone Gate** — Language is analytical, avoids advocacy framing, presents findings as evidence
3. **Legal/Sensitivity Gate** — No personally identifiable data; no characterizations of named individuals
4. **Leadership Sign-Off** — Organizational leader reviews final draft and authorizes release

### Hour 66-72: Distribution

- Format to organizational letterhead (PDF and web-ready HTML)
- Distribute to all list categories simultaneously
- Post to website and social channels with summary excerpt
- Log brief in Policy Brief Archive with metadata tags

| Category | Recipients | Format |
|---|---|---|
| **Elected Officials** | City council, county board, state legislators | PDF via email, printed copies for in-person |
| **Media** | Local reporters covering relevant beats; editorial boards | PDF + 1-paragraph press summary |
| **Funders** | Current and prospective foundation partners | PDF via email |
| **Coalition Partners** | Community-based organizations, advocacy groups | PDF + plain-language summary |
| **Academic/Research** | University faculty, peer CDCs | PDF via email |

## Branding Standards

**Header:** Organization logo, full name, tagline

**Footer:** Address, website, contact email, brief ID number (format: [ORG]-PB-YYYY-NNN)

**Citation Format:**
> [Organization Name]. (Year). [Brief Title]. Policy Brief [ORG]-PB-YYYY-NNN. [City, State]: [Organization Name].

**Disclaimer (required on all briefs):**
> This policy brief is produced for informational purposes. Data are drawn from publicly available sources and the organization's research infrastructure. This brief does not constitute legal advice or a formal organizational endorsement of any pending legislation. For methodology questions, contact [research email].

**Data Attribution:**
> Data sourced from [Organization]'s equity data infrastructure, U.S. Census Bureau American Community Survey, [additional sources as applicable].

---

## Quality Standards

- Maximum 2 pages (800-1,000 words excluding tables)
- At least one data table or chart description per brief
- All statistics cite a specific source and year
- No jargon without definition; accessible to a general audience
- Equity implications section required in every brief
- Methodology note required in every brief
